Virtual Reality Development

WCBVR

WCBVR

Project Goal

Our challenge was making WCBVR feel realistic but still making it accessible to the player. Essentially, making sure our virtual reality experience was as close to wheelchair athletes experience as possible. This included making sure the wheelchair itself behaved like it would in real life. We had to study how players turned, their speed (both forward and backward), and the momentum behind shooting in order to transfer it into our virtual world.

  • Category Virtual Reality Development
  • Date 2018-2019
  • Client STRAPS & Spurs Give

Designing and developing
with accessibility at the forefront

I developed the game using Unity for Oculus Rift. In order to create a more realistic experience, we partnered with RACTIVE, another local San Antonio company, for motion capture that we later integrated into the game. We play tested the game with both able and non-able bodied individuals to ensure accessibility throughout its development.

I was the lead programmer on the project. My focus was on the physics interactions, animation programming and optimization of the game. We found early on that using purely realistic physics for the player was not fun. So, I developed a dynamic way to assist the player dependent on if they were shooting or passing and their individual skill level.

I was also in the process of developing non-player controlled player AI using behavior trees. However, in testing with some of our players, it proved to risk the game's goal of accessibility, so it was dropped. The game is in early access, in case we continue development on that mode in the future.
